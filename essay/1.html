In the Beginning There Were Five: An Introduction

With the Oxford English Dictionary adding the phrases 'binge-watch' and 'spoiler alert' to its repertoire in June 2018, it's clear that the television landscape is transforming.

To understand exactly how, we have to go back to the very basics. Television has existed from the very start with one simple goal in mind - to sell people products.

The very first television commercial aired on June 1st, 1941 for the Bulova Watch Co. The 10-second commercial reportedly cost $9 at the time, which is around $500 adjusted for inflation, making it a bargain by today's standards.

In recent years, some companies have paid upwards of $5 million for advertisements during the annual US Super Bowl sporting event - paying for the privilege of promoting their product to an audience of around 100 million.
